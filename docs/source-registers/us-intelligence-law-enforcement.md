# Official U.S. Intelligence And Law Enforcement Source Register

**UNCLASSIFIED//OPEN SOURCE**

**Source Register ID:** WI-SOURCE-REGISTER-USILE-2026-0001

**Prepared UTC:** 2026-06-13T05:56:38Z

**Information cutoff UTC:** 2026-06-13T05:56:38Z

**Purpose:** Organize official U.S. intelligence, homeland security, law
enforcement, sanctions, border, cyber, counterterrorism, counternarcotics, and
geospatial source families for WARLOCK-INDEX strategic threat analysis.

**Boundary:** This register supports public strategic research only. It does
not provide recommendations, targeting support, collection tasking,
investigative direction, operational planning, enforcement guidance, tactical
instructions, surveillance guidance, informant handling, route selection,
technical exploitation, or domestic political profiling.

## Framing Rule

WARLOCK-INDEX does not create a political "enemies list." This register uses
official public threat categories: foreign states, foreign intelligence
services, terrorist organizations, nonstate armed networks, transnational
criminal organizations, cyber actors, sanctions targets, border and maritime
threat vectors, weapons of mass destruction concerns, and domestic terrorism
or domestic violent extremism where official sources define those categories.

Domestic threat coverage must remain tied to violence, criminal conduct,
official public definitions, and civil liberties boundaries. It must not treat
lawful dissent, protected speech, religion, ethnicity, nationality, political
identity, journalism, protest, or association as an intelligence target.

## Core Official Source Families

### ODNI Annual Threat Assessment

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Office of the Director of National Intelligence
- **Current repository anchor:** ODNI Annual Threat Assessment 2026
- **URL:** https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/assessments/ATA-2026-Unclassified-Report.pdf
- **Use:** Public Intelligence Community baseline for foreign adversaries,
  terrorism, cyber, space, WMD, regional instability, and homeland-relevant
  threat framing.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for public IC framing. It is a public
  assessment with release constraints and should be cross-read with agency
  source packets for domain detail.

### CIA World Leaders

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Central Intelligence Agency
- **Publication cadence:** CIA states that the directory is updated weekly.
- **URL:** https://www.cia.gov/resources/world-leaders/
- **Use:** Foreign government leadership, cabinet, ambassador, and permanent
  representative reference source.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative public CIA reference for foreign
  leadership directory use. It is not a threat assessment and should not be
  converted into targeting or personal dossiers.

### CIA World Factbook And CIA Maps Legacy Source

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Central Intelligence Agency
- **Status note:** CIA announced in February 2026 that The World Factbook had
  sunset. It remains useful as legacy context only where archived material is
  available and where a dated product can identify the cutoff.
- **URL:** https://www.cia.gov/the-world-factbook/
- **Use:** Legacy country, geography, demographic, economic, and map context;
  current country facts should be refreshed through State Department, CIA
  World Leaders, ODNI, DoD, Commerce, Treasury, Census, USGS, or other current
  official sources.
- **Reliability note:** Strong historical reference value, but not a current
  country database after sunset. Maps are strategic reference artifacts only;
  WARLOCK-INDEX does not use maps for targeting or operational planning.
- **Replacement note:** Use the Legacy Factbook Replacement Note for current
  field-by-field source routing.

### FBI Terrorism Program Public Page

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Federal Bureau of Investigation
- **URL:** https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/terrorism
- **Use:** FBI public framing for terrorism, international terrorism,
  domestic terrorism, Joint Terrorism Task Force context, threat evolution,
  and public definitions.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for FBI public law-enforcement framing.
  Domestic coverage must distinguish violent criminal conduct from protected
  expression and association.

### FBI Counterintelligence And Espionage

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Federal Bureau of Investigation
- **URL:** https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/counterintelligence
- **Use:** Public FBI framing for foreign intelligence activity, espionage,
  economic espionage, malign foreign influence, insider threat, and
  counterintelligence risk.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for FBI public mission framing. It does
  not authorize WARLOCK-INDEX to identify persons, organizations, or
  communities as intelligence threats absent official public evidence.

### FBI Cyber

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Federal Bureau of Investigation
- **URL:** https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/cyber
- **Use:** Public FBI cyber threat framing, cybercrime categories, ransomware,
  cyber-enabled fraud, foreign cyber actor context, and reporting pathways.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for FBI public cyber mission framing.
  WARLOCK-INDEX uses it for strategic categorization and does not reproduce
  exploit steps, evasion methods, malware procedures, or investigative tactics.

### FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center
- **URL:** https://www.ic3.gov/Media/PDF/AnnualReport/2024_IC3Report.pdf
- **Use:** Cybercrime reporting and annual loss/complaint trend baseline,
  including fraud, ransomware, business email compromise, extortion, and
  victim-reporting categories.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for IC3 public complaint data. Complaint
  data is not a complete measurement of all cybercrime and should be labeled
  as reported activity.

### FBI Weapons Of Mass Destruction

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Federal Bureau of Investigation
- **URL:** https://www.fbi.gov/investigate/wmd
- **Use:** Public law-enforcement framing for WMD terrorism, chemical,
  biological, radiological, nuclear, and explosive threat prevention at
  strategic level.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for FBI public framing. WARLOCK-INDEX
  must not provide materials, methods, recipes, deployment guidance, or
  vulnerability exploitation detail.

### NCTC Terrorist Groups Public Guide

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** National Counterterrorism Center / ODNI
- **URL:** https://www.dni.gov/nctc/groups.html
- **Use:** Public historical counterterrorism group reference and state
  sponsor context.
- **Reliability note:** The page states that its information was valid as of
  2015. Use as historical baseline only and refresh current status through
  State, Treasury, ODNI, FBI, DHS, UN, allied, and research sources. Do not
  reproduce tactics or operational method detail.

### DHS Homeland Threat Assessment Product Family

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of Homeland Security
- **URL:** https://www.dhs.gov/homeland-threat-assessment
- **Use:** Homeland threat framing involving terrorism, targeted violence,
  border security, illicit drugs, cyber, election security, critical
  infrastructure, natural hazards, and foreign influence where publicly
  released.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for DHS public framing. Current URL and
  PDF paths should be refreshed during each dated source-packet update because
  DHS pages are dynamic and sometimes protected by content delivery controls.

### DHS Office Of Intelligence And Analysis

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of Homeland Security
- **URL:** https://www.dhs.gov/office-intelligence-and-analysis
- **Use:** DHS intelligence mission framing, homeland threat environment, and
  coordination with federal, state, local, tribal, territorial, and private
  partners.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for DHS public organizational framing.
  WARLOCK-INDEX must avoid sensitive collection, fusion center, or partner
  operational detail.

### CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities Catalog

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency
- **URL:** https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog
- **Use:** Public exploited-vulnerability reference for strategic cyber risk,
  patch-priority awareness, and source-class mapping.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative CISA catalog. WARLOCK-INDEX may use it
  for high-level vulnerability trend analysis, not exploit instructions,
  weaponization, evasion, scanning, or targeting.

### CISA Cybersecurity Advisories

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency
- **URL:** https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories
- **Use:** Public cyber advisory source family, including joint advisories
  with NSA, FBI, and allied agencies.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative advisory source. Technical detail should
  be summarized only at defensive strategic level; do not reproduce exploit
  paths or operational playbooks.

### NSA Cybersecurity Advisories

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** National Security Agency
- **URL:** https://www.nsa.gov/Press-Room/Cybersecurity-Advisories-Guidance/
- **Use:** Public NSA cyber defense guidance and joint advisory source family.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for public NSA defensive guidance.
  WARLOCK-INDEX should extract actor, sector, and mitigation themes without
  turning advisories into exploit manuals.

### State Department Foreign Terrorist Organizations

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of State
- **URL:** https://www.state.gov/foreign-terrorist-organizations/
- **Use:** Official FTO designation list and legal-public baseline for
  designated foreign terrorist organizations.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for State designation status. Use with
  NCTC, Treasury, FBI, DHS, UN, and allied sources for broader context.

### State Department Country Reports On Terrorism

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of State
- **URL:** https://www.state.gov/country-reports-on-terrorism/
- **Use:** Annual country and regional public reporting on terrorism,
  counterterrorism cooperation, foreign terrorist organizations, and terrorist
  safe haven context.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative State public reporting. It reflects U.S.
  policy and diplomatic perspective and should be cross-read with UN and
  allied sources.

### Treasury OFAC Sanctions Lists And Risk Assessments

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of the Treasury
- **URLs:** https://ofac.treasury.gov/ ; https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/terrorism-and-illicit-finance
- **Use:** Sanctions, terrorist financing, money laundering, proliferation
  finance, cyber sanctions, narcotics finance, corruption, and illicit finance
  source family.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for Treasury public sanctions and risk
  framing. WARLOCK-INDEX must not provide sanctions evasion or compliance
  circumvention guidance.

### DEA National Drug Threat Assessment

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Drug Enforcement Administration
- **URL:** https://www.dea.gov/
- **Use:** Official U.S. law-enforcement source family for drug trafficking,
  synthetic opioids, cartels, illicit finance, precursor supply chains, and
  domestic public safety effects.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for DEA public threat framing. WARLOCK-INDEX should use it for strategic source mapping, not trafficking methods,
  concealment, production, or distribution guidance.

### ATF Firearms Commerce And Trafficking Sources

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives
- **URL:** https://www.atf.gov/resource-center/data-statistics
- **Use:** Firearms commerce, tracing, trafficking, explosives, arson, and
  criminal gun intelligence source family at public statistical level.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for ATF public statistics and program
  framing. Do not convert tracing or explosives material into investigative,
  procurement, trafficking, or device guidance.

### CBP Border Statistics

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection
- **URL:** https://www.cbp.gov/newsroom/stats
- **Use:** Border encounters, seizures, migration, trade enforcement,
  transnational criminal organization pressure, and port-of-entry source
  family.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for CBP public statistics. Statistics
  require careful definitions and do not by themselves prove criminality,
  intent, nationality-level threat, or individual risk.

### U.S. Coast Guard Maritime Security Sources

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Coast Guard
- **URL:** https://www.uscg.mil/
- **Use:** Maritime security, port security, counter-drug interdiction public
  reporting, Arctic maritime, illegal fishing, and homeland maritime source
  family.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for Coast Guard public framing. Avoid
  patrol patterns, interdiction tactics, vessel targeting, or sensitive
  movement analysis.

### DOJ National Security Division

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** U.S. Department of Justice
- **URL:** https://www.justice.gov/nsd
- **Use:** Public legal case, counterintelligence, counterterrorism, export
  control, sanctions, foreign agent, and cyber prosecution source family.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for public filings and DOJ statements.
  Legal cases are allegation- and evidence-specific and should not be
  generalized to communities, protected classes, or political categories.

### National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency Public Sources

- **Source class:** A
- **Publisher:** National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency
- **URL:** https://www.nga.mil/
- **Use:** Public geospatial intelligence, maps, maritime safety, and open
  reference source family where released.
- **Reliability note:** Authoritative for public NGA material. WARLOCK-INDEX
  uses maps only for strategic orientation and not for targeting,
  surveillance, facility vulnerability analysis, or operational movement.
