**Source base:** ODNI, CIA, FBI, NCTC, DHS, CISA, NSA, State, Treasury, DEA,
ATF, CBP, Coast Guard, DOJ, NGA, and existing WARLOCK-INDEX official-source
products, including the U.S. law-enforcement threat source capture packet,
U.S. law-enforcement threat source assessment, Salt Typhoon and
telecommunications defensive source note, ASEAN/claimant-state South China
Sea cross-check packet, and U.S.-Iran MOU source-treatment note.

**Analytic confidence:** High for organizing logic; moderate for current
source completeness pending dated source packets for each agency family.

**Purpose:** Connect official U.S. agency source families to WARLOCK-INDEX
threat categories and safety boundaries.

**Boundary:** This matrix does not produce an enemy list, targeting map,
investigative guide, cyber playbook, enforcement guide, or domestic political
threat profile.

## Bottom Line

Official U.S. threat coverage belongs in a matrix because no single agency
source family is sufficient. ODNI provides the IC-wide frame; CIA provides
foreign reference material; FBI and DHS provide domestic and homeland
law-enforcement framing; CISA/NSA/FBI provide cyber advisory streams; State
and Treasury provide designations, sanctions, and terrorism finance; DEA,
ATF, CBP, Coast Guard, and DOJ provide transnational crime, border, maritime,
firearms, narcotics, and legal-case source streams; NGA and CIA maps provide
geospatial reference at strategic level only.

## Cross-Source Discipline

| Evidence type | Examples | Strength | Caution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Intelligence assessment | ODNI ATA, DIA military power reports | Strong public strategic baseline | Public release constraints |
| Declassified disclosure | ODNI public releases and linked declassified artifacts | Strong for issuer perspective and source-event provenance | Underlying claims require corroboration; granular biological and facility details may be unsafe to reproduce |
| Legal designation | State FTO, Treasury OFAC, Federal Register, DOJ cases | Strong for official status | Legal framing is not full threat analysis |
| Law-enforcement program page | FBI, DEA, ATF, CBP, Coast Guard pages | Strong for mission and source routing | Often not an analytic report |
| Public statistics | IC3, CBP stats, ATF data, DEA reports | Useful trend support | Definitions and reporting bias matter |
| Cyber advisory | CISA, NSA, FBI advisories | Strong defensive source | Technical detail must be summarized safely |
| Map/geospatial reference | CIA maps, NGA, DoD AOR maps | Useful orientation | No targeting or vulnerability use |

## Information Gaps

- The U.S. law-enforcement source capture packet and assessment now establish
  the FBI/DHS/DOJ/DEA/ATF/CBP/Coast Guard/Treasury/State/IC3 routing model,
  but dated page-level packets are still needed for CISA/NSA/FBI cyber,
  Treasury sanctions, DEA/ATF/CBP/Coast Guard TCO, DOJ NSD legal actions,
  DoD Cooperative Threat Reduction, BWC treaty and compliance, select-agent
  oversight, and emerging biotechnology security.
- China/PLA source collection now has a broad baseline, PRC issuer-language
  packet, DoD/DIA military-power extraction map, and tracker, but dedicated
  PRC cyber, Taiwan pressure, South China Sea, ASEAN/claimant-state regional
  cross-check, PLA services/arms, defense-industrial, DoD-to-PRC-issuer
  crosswalk, and Salt Typhoon telecom source treatment; South China Sea
  page-level, advisory page-level, FCC, OFAC, and allied cyber cross-check
  refreshes remain follow-on lanes.
- Agency product pages change frequently and require dated refreshes.
- The U.S.-Iran MOU lane currently has transcript reporting and secondary
  reporting, but no directly retrieved official published text, Treasury/OFAC
  waiver, IAEA record, or UN record in this pass.
- Public sources cannot replace classified assessments, investigative records,
  or sensitive operational reporting.
- The Ukraine biolab claim-reconciliation note now preserves issuer layering
  for ODNI, DoD, State/BWC, Ukrainian, UN, and contested state-source lanes,
  but document-level DoD Cooperative Threat Reduction, State Department
  arms-control/BWC, congressional, Ukrainian, treaty-organization, and
  technical biosecurity sources remain needed before the corpus makes stronger
  judgments.
- Domestic threat lanes need recurring civil-liberties review.
