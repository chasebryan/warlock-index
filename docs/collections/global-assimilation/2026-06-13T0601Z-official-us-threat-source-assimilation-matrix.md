# Official U.S. Threat Source Assimilation Matrix

**Classification:** UNCLASSIFIED//OPEN SOURCE

**Matrix ID:** WI-MATRIX-US-THREAT-SOURCES-2026-0001

**Prepared UTC:** 2026-06-13T06:01:00Z

**Information cutoff UTC:** 2026-06-13T06:01:00Z

**Source base:** ODNI, CIA, FBI, NCTC, DHS, CISA, NSA, State, Treasury, DEA,
ATF, CBP, Coast Guard, DOJ, NGA, and existing Warlock Index official-source
products.

**Analytic confidence:** High for organizing logic; moderate for current
source completeness pending dated source packets for each agency family.

**Purpose:** Connect official U.S. agency source families to Warlock Index
threat categories and safety boundaries.

**Boundary:** This matrix does not produce an enemy list, targeting map,
investigative guide, cyber playbook, enforcement guide, or domestic political
threat profile.

## Bottom Line

Official U.S. threat coverage belongs in a matrix because no single agency
source family is sufficient. ODNI provides the IC-wide frame; CIA provides
foreign reference material; FBI and DHS provide domestic and homeland
law-enforcement framing; CISA/NSA/FBI provide cyber advisory streams; State
and Treasury provide designations, sanctions, and terrorism finance; DEA,
ATF, CBP, Coast Guard, and DOJ provide transnational crime, border, maritime,
firearms, narcotics, and legal-case source streams; NGA and CIA maps provide
geospatial reference at strategic level only.

## Information Gaps

- Dedicated source packets are still needed for FBI/DHS, CISA/NSA/FBI cyber,
  CIA/State/NGA reference, Treasury sanctions, DEA/ATF/CBP/Coast Guard TCO,
  and DOJ NSD legal actions.
- Agency product pages change frequently and require dated refreshes.
- Public sources cannot replace classified assessments, investigative records,
  or sensitive operational reporting.
- Domestic threat lanes need recurring civil-liberties review.
