## Bottom Line

The U.S.-Iran MOU lane now has enough public reporting to warrant a dedicated
implementation-capture packet, but not enough official-source evidence to
treat the reported text as an implemented legal or operational settlement.
Axios provides transcript-style text attributed to a senior administration
official's readout. Guardian live reporting now says Trump and Pezeshkian
signed the MOU, Iranian state media released text largely tracking the U.S.
readout, and mediator Pakistan said the agreement took immediate effect, while
Washington still had not formally released the text. Guardian, New York Post,
Financial Times, and other reporting describe signing/status claims, Hormuz
terms, sanctions-waiver claims, nuclear/IAEA provisions, and political
reaction.

WARLOCK-INDEX should treat the MOU as a multi-layer evidence problem:
reported text, official release, signing instrument, Treasury/OFAC waivers,
IAEA safeguards records, UN Security Council action, Hormuz maritime
implementation, energy-market behavior, Iranian official interpretation, and
mediator statements are distinct source families.

## Packet Use Rules

1. Use the Axios transcript as reported text only and label it as transcript
   reporting whenever clause-level language is discussed.
2. Do not treat reported Treasury waivers, sanctions termination, asset
   access, or oil-export provisions as implemented unless Treasury/OFAC,
   Federal Register, State, banking/shipping notices, or legal records are
   directly captured.
3. Do not treat reported nuclear/IAEA provisions as verified unless IAEA, UN,
   State, Iranian, or final-deal records support them.
4. Keep Strait of Hormuz handling strategic. Do not provide routes, vessel
   guidance, toll-avoidance logic, de-mining detail, naval activity, or live
   maritime assessment.
5. Separate ceasefire, Lebanon/Hezbollah, Israel, Red Sea, and Gulf claims.
6. Record official absence as an information gap, not as proof that the MOU is
   invalid or unimplemented.
