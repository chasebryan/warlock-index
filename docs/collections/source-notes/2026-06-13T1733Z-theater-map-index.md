# Theater Map Index

**UNCLASSIFIED//OPEN SOURCE**

**Product ID:** WI-MAPINDEX-THEATER-2026-0001

**Prepared UTC:** 2026-06-13T17:33:00Z

**Information cutoff UTC:** 2026-06-13T17:33:00Z

**Purpose:** Link Warlock-Index theater lanes to safe public map,
geospatial, command-area, nautical, topographic, and naming-source families.

**Boundary:** This index supports strategic orientation and source routing
only. It does not create targeting maps, sensitive facility overlays,
infrastructure vulnerability maps, live movement layers, route-selection
tools, patrol-pattern analysis, collection geometry, interdiction guidance,
operational planning, surveillance products, or investigative direction.

## Bottom Line

Theater maps in Warlock-Index should answer a narrow question: which public
source family can orient a reader to a theater without enabling operational
misuse? The answer is different by lane. NGA public-release maps are the
first stop for global and selected theater orientation. Combatant command
area-of-responsibility pages can show U.S. command-area framing. USGS, NOAA,
and the U.S. Board on Geographic Names cover U.S. topographic, maritime, and
naming layers. NATO public map and member-country pages support alliance
geography. State, foreign government, UN, and allied maps can add diplomatic
or issuer-perspective context after dated refresh.

This index does not bless every public map for Warlock-Index use. It points
future products to safe source families and states what each family cannot be
used to do.

## Universal Map Rules

1. Label every map as static, current, historical, nautical, topographic,
   command-area, naming-source, or issuer-perspective material.
2. Record publisher, URL, access date, title, publication or release date
   where available, and naming or boundary caveats.
3. Use maps for orientation, provenance, and source routing only.
4. Do not add tactical overlays, sensitive facilities, likely routes,
   collection geometry, sensor coverage, live movement, vulnerability labels,
   or interdiction points.
5. Do not treat a map as proof of sovereignty, control, threat intent,
   basing, force posture, or operational feasibility without separate
   non-map evidence.

## Theater Product Checklist

Before a theater product links or embeds a map, verify:

- The map is public and official or clearly labeled as a non-official source.
- The product needs orientation rather than operational detail.
- The map has no added tactical overlays from Warlock-Index.
- The product states publisher, URL, access date, title, and release date
  where available.
- Naming, boundary, and recognition caveats are explicit.
- The product includes a boundary note if the map involves contested
  geography, combatant command AORs, nautical charts, ports, critical
  infrastructure, military geography, or domestic U.S. locations.

## Source Family Limits

### NGA Public Maps

Use NGA public maps as static orientation products and source-provenance
anchors. Do not infer current operations, targeting, control, basing, or
vulnerability from them.

### Combatant Command AOR Pages

Use command AOR pages to show public U.S. command-area framing. Do not infer
force posture, access rights, current missions, basing, patrol patterns, or
operational priorities from AOR maps alone.

### USGS And NOAA

Use USGS and NOAA for U.S. topographic, nautical, hydrographic, and coastal
source routing. Do not convert public datasets into domestic surveillance,
facility vulnerability, route-selection, or exploitation products.

### NATO And Allied Maps

Use NATO and allied maps for alliance geography and issuer-perspective
framing. Preserve issuer labels and do not treat alliance map display as
proof of operational control, national capability, or delivered force.

### State, UN, Foreign Government, And Multilateral Maps

Use diplomatic, UN, foreign-government, and multilateral maps for issuer
perspective, recognition language, boundary caveats, and humanitarian or
development context. Do not convert them into operational movement,
targeting, or vulnerability products.

## Information Gaps

- The index does not yet include exact safe map anchors for every country or
  subregion. Future theater products should add dated map-specific source
  packets when a lane becomes map-heavy.
- State country and area pages require manual refresh before current dated
  use in this environment.
- Some official maps are interactive or JavaScript-dependent. Products should
  preserve access limitations and avoid silently extracting unsupported map
  layers.
- Public maps can encode policy, naming, boundary, projection, recognition,
  or source-age choices. Products should state those caveats rather than
  flattening them into Warlock-Index judgments.
