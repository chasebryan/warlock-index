**UNCLASSIFIED//OPEN SOURCE**

**Information cutoff UTC:** 2026-06-18T04:41:47Z

**Source base:** WARLOCK-INDEX public site footer, About page, generated
documentation footer template, RSS stylesheet footer, workspace footer, and
existing repository metadata.

**Analytic confidence:** High for internal site attribution treatment and
footer wording. Low for any interpretation that implies a registered legal
entity, nonprofit, foundation, institute, company, or formal organization.

**Purpose:** Preserve an explicit corpus note that WARLOCK-INDEX is maintained
as an independent open-source research project and should not present itself
as a foundation or registered organization unless that legal entity exists and
the maintainer chooses to use it.

**Boundary:** Attribution and source-treatment only. This note does not create
an organization, legal entity, nonprofit, foundation, business, partnership,
tax status, governance body, editorial board, or institutional endorsement.

## Bottom Line

WARLOCK-INDEX should use project-based attribution. The site should not use
foundation-style maintainer wording because that phrase can imply a
registered foundation or organization. The footer should use the exact wording
"Maintained by Chase Bryan." The About page can describe WARLOCK-INDEX as an
independent open-source research corpus maintained by Chase Bryan.

This keeps the corpus transparent without creating a false institutional
signal. It also preserves the distinction between a public research project,
its maintainer, and any future legal or organizational structure that might or
might not exist.

## Treatment Rules

1. Do not use "foundation", "institute", "organization", "company",
   "nonprofit", or similar entity language unless there is a deliberate legal
   and operational reason to do so.
2. Prefer "independent open-source research project" when describing the site
   as a whole.
3. Use "maintained by Chase Bryan" when a human maintainer needs to be named.
4. Keep source products focused on source provenance, scope, confidence, and
   caveats rather than institutional self-branding.
5. If a future legal entity is created, add a dated source-treatment note
   before changing site-wide attribution language.
