## Standing Classification

China / PRC is classified for WARLOCK-INDEX purposes as:

**Principal U.S. peer competitor; CCP party-state; whole-of-nation PLA
modernization challenge; primary Taiwan flashpoint actor; persistent
homeland-relevant cyber threat; space-enabled and counterspace-capable great
power; expanding strategic nuclear competitor; maritime coercion actor;
dual-use techno-industrial competitor; selective cross-theater adversary
alignment actor.**

## Core Actor Notes

- China is the only state actor currently able to challenge U.S. power across
  military, economic, technological, industrial, diplomatic, cyber, space, and
  information domains at global scale.
- CCP control, national rejuvenation, economic development, and sovereignty
  claims are interlocked in Beijing's strategic logic.
- The PLA is modernizing for regional advantage along the First Island Chain
  while building capabilities that affect U.S. homeland, allied, and global
  interests.
- Taiwan is the most acute flashpoint, but China analysis must also cover
  cyber, space, nuclear, maritime, economic, technology, and influence domains.
- China's support to Russia's defense industrial base gives the PRC a
  cross-theater relevance beyond the Indo-Pacific.
